AR Living Mural.

In this 2-quarter capstone project for my major, I was chosen by my peers as the project lead and scrum master of the augmented reality team. I led a multi-disciplinary team in the developement of a real-world product, commissioned by the city of Sacramento. In working together with historians, artists, city officials, and developers, we created a prototype of an AR scavenger hunt as part of a larger effort to revitalize the K-Street Tunnel (a key passageway connecting historical Sacramento with it's newer developments). This demo shows an example of scaled down AR-interactive sections of a large mural that spans the length of the passageway.

Family Tree.

This game was created during Global Game Jam 2019. It was made in 48 hours by a team of myself and 3 other game developers. Dont let the friendly graphics fool you, this is not an easy game. In this multi-styled platformer you will learn to use your wings, find a mate, fight off threats to your lover, and return home to your nest to complete the cycle of life. Different mates come with different traits, which are passed down to future generations. But don't be too picky, or else your family tree may end with you!

Text Adventure!

A throwback to digital games in their earliest form. If you have never played a text based game before, I recommend checking out the "IF Postcard" link (after clicking "Play in Browser") for a quick reference guide to the controls. Try to be as descriptive as possible when interacting with objects in the game. For example: instead of typing "flip switch," try saying "turn the red switch on." Enjoy!
